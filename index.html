Zoo Animals

10 Amazing Facts about Zoo Animals

Bears

  1. Ollie

    Ollie is an North American Grizzly Bear and weighs up to 800 pounds. Ollie is native to the state of Washington. Ollie has thick brown fur to endure the cold weather and long claws for digging.

    Ollie the Grizzly
  2. Mona

    Mona is an American Black bear native to the state of Tennesse. Mona has a thick black coat with many layers to keep warm during the winter months, and short claws essential for climbing. Mona has a great sense of smell, and often can be seen on her hind legs sniffing scents.

    Mona the Black Bear

Giraffes

  1. Frankie

    Frankie is a Northern Giraffe native out of East Africa. Frankie runs about 35mph and has a plant based diet. Northern Giraffes are also known as the three horned Giraffe.

    Frankie Northern Giraffe
  2. Coconut

    Coconut is an Masai Giraffe native from Kenya. Coconut eats around 16-20 hours a day and may eat up to 75 pounds of leaves a day.

    Coconut Masai Giraffe

Lion

  1. Mella

    Mella is a West African Lion! Mella plays an important role in her herd call a pride. Mella is female lion, which are called Lioness. Lioness are the prides primary hunters and leaders.

    Mella the Lion
  2. Karl

    Karl is male African lion that weighs about 500 pounds. Male lions tend to grow amazing manes as they grow older. Also, male lions eat first after the lioness kills the prey.

    Karl the Male Lion

    3. Monkeys

    1. Cookie

      Cookie is a Spider Monkey. Spider monkeys usually live in a group of 20-30. Spider monkeys have long, thin arms with hooklike hands that allow them to swing through the trees. They do not have opposable thumbs.

      Cookie Spider Monkey
    2. Earl

      Earl is a Marmoset, also known as a Finger Monkey. Marmosets are small monkeys that live high up in the canopies of South American rainforests. There are more than 20 species, and most could fit comfortably in an adult human's hand

      Earl the Marmoset
    3. Banana Pudding

      Banna Pudding is a Gorilla, the largest great ape. Female Gorillas can weigh up to 215 pounds. While gorillas are able to stand upright, they prefer to walk using their hands and their legs.

      Banana Pudding Gorilla

    Alligators

    1. Wren

      Wren is a American Albino Alligator. The white color makes Wren very rare in the wild. The white color is caused by a genetic condition called Albinoism.

      Albino Gators
    2. Aspen

      Aspen is a Chinese Alligator also known as the Yangtze Alligator. Dark gray or black in color with a fully armored body, the Chinese alligator grows to 1.5–2.1 metres (5–7 ft) in length and weighs 36–45 kilograms (80–100 lb) as an adult. It brumates in burrows in winter and is nocturnal in summer.

      Chinese Gator
    3. Mika

      Mika is an American Alligator born two weeks ago. Baby alligators too small in size to snack on large creatures, but their tongues don’t move much right away, so they stick to smaller “treats” like flies, baby shrimp, worms and snails.

      Mika the Gator
