10 Amazing Facts about Zoo Animals

  • Bears

    1. Ollie

      2. Ollie is an North American Grizzly Bear and weighs up to 800 pounds. Ollie is native to the state of Washington. Ollie has thick brown fur to endure the cold weather and long claws for digging.

      Ollie the Grizzly
    2. Mona

      3. Mona is an American Black bear native to the state of Tennesse. Mona has a thick black coat with many layers to keep warm during the winter months, and short claws essential for climbing. Mona has a great sense of smell, and often can be seen on her hind legs sniffing scents.

      Mona the Black Bear

  • Giraffes

    1. Frankie

      Frankie is a Northern Giraffe native out of East Africa. Frankie runs about 35mph and has a plant based diet. Northern Giraffes are also known as the three horned Giraffe.

      Frankie Northern Giraffe
    2. Coconut

      3. Coconut is an Masai Giraffe native from Kenya. Coconut eats around 16-20 hours a day and may eat up to 75 pounds of leaves a day.

      Coconut Masai Giraffe

    1. Mella

      Mella is a West African Lion! Mella plays an important role in her herd call a pride. Mella is female lion, which are called Lioness. Lioness are the prides primary hunters and leaders.
